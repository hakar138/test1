How Sound Travels Through Different Media

Sound is a mechanical wave that requires a medium to travel. The speed of sound depends on the properties of the medium, such as its density and elasticity.

Same Frequency

When sound travels through different media, its frequency remains constant. Your ear hears the same pitch regardless of the medium.

Changing Wavelength

Since wave speed = frequency Ã— wavelength, when speed changes and frequency stays constant, the wavelength must change.

Sound travels slowest in gases, faster in liquids, and fastest in solids. This shows how the medium affects the wave speed while the frequency remains constant.
